Blueprint Wireframe

01
Idea: Technical drawing. A is drafted with dashed construction lines, apex crosshair, height dimension (H), and revision/sheet numbers — like a real architectural sheet you'd find in a set of plans.

Roman Inscription

02
Idea: Trajan-style capitals referencing Roman monumental inscriptions (Arch of Titus, Pantheon). Latin motto "Qvi aedificat web" = "He who builds the web." Classical authority.

Pitched Roof Glyph

03
Idea: Just a roof silhouette sitting on a foundation line — simultaneously A and house. Minimal, suggestive, not literal. No windows, no walls. The viewer completes the house.

Skyscraper

04
Idea: A tapering tower with horizontal window bands + antenna spire. Tapers outward like an A. "We build skyscrapers, not huts." Ambitious, urban, permanent.

Compass

05
Idea: Drafting compass — pivot at apex, arc being drawn at base, crossbar = the drawn line. Tool of the craft. Pivot = emphasis (the accent point).

Isometric Block

06
Idea: Single 3D prism viewed from 3/4 angle with crossbar groove cut into the top face. Clean, architectural, solid. Reads as "we build dimensional things."

Modular Blocks v3

07
Idea: Mixed-size blocks with subtle depth shading + one highlighted "keystone" block at the crossbar in bright cyan. Modular composition — we build from pieces.

Truss / Constellation

08
Idea: Engineering truss with cross-bracing and riveted joints. A as load-bearing structure. Signals "we engineer systems, not just decoration."

Masthead Seal

09
Idea: Editorial masthead: circular seal with A + laurel ticks + MMXXVI date, stacked ARCHI/FEX with top & bottom rules and Latin motto. Full brand system.

Architectural Drawing

10
Idea: Full CAD drawing treatment — [A] bracket icon, horizontal dimension line under the wordmark, vertical dimension on the side. Annotated like a technical drawing.

Isometric Cubes

11
Idea: A built from 10 isometric unit cubes — the platonic ideal of "built from blocks." Modular + dimensional + honest about construction.

Exploded Construction v2

12
Idea: Three parts — apex / crossbar / legs — separated as if mid-assembly. Shows WE understand construction. Cleaner alignment than v1, faint axis guide replaces busy dashes.

Wireframe Isometric

13
Idea: See-through 3D — every edge visible, no fills. Engineering model meets blueprint. Transparency = "we show our work."

Keystone Arch v2

14
Idea: Simplified arch — just pillars + curve + keystone, no door or windows. Keystone A is integrated INTO the arch apex (not floating). "Keystone of your stack."

Process (Single A)

15
Idea: ONE A, sliced vertically into thirds: left = sketched, middle = blueprint dashed, right = solid built. Shows the full lifecycle (idea → blueprint → built) in a single mark.

Scaffold v2

16
Idea: Solid A wrapped in real scaffolding — thick pipes, diagonal braces, bolted joints, red safety flag. Construction is underway. Much more "jobsite" than v1.

Roof Monogram v2

17
Idea: Just the roof + foundation. A-shape peak with crossbar as a visible rafter. No doors, no windows, no walls. The viewer fills in "house."

Carved Stone v2

18
Idea & meaning: "Archifex" is neo-Latin. ARCHI- (Greek: chief/master) + -FEX (Latin from facere, to make) — parallel to artifex (artisan), pontifex (bridge-maker → pontiff), opifex (craftsman). Literal meaning: chief maker / master builder. Inscription style references how Romans carved these words into monumental stone.

Tessellation v2

19
Idea: Triangular tiles tessellate into an A, then FADE outward (suggesting infinite scale). Highlighted bright keystone tile at the crossbar = "every system has a load-bearing element." "Modular by design."

AF Ligature

20
Idea: A and F fused into a single typographic mark — they share the crossbar. Archi+Fex concept rendered as an actual ligature. Elegant, literary, distinctive at small sizes.

Construction Crane

21
Idea: Solid A with tower crane rising from the apex — boom, cables, hook. Reads as "actively building." A + construction site in one glyph.

Rebar Framework

22
Idea: Reinforcement steel (rebar) before the concrete pour. Diagonals + horizontal ties + tie-wire dots at intersections. Shows "we build structures that hold."

Bauhaus

23
Idea: Circle + triangle + rectangle + dot — the Bauhaus primary shapes composing an A. "Form follows function." Movement that defined modern architecture.

Monolith

24
Idea: Thick, carved A-slab with depth shadow + etched crossbar groove + edge scale ticks. Feels like it was cut from a single block of stone. Permanent, weighty.

Paper Fold

25
Idea: A as folded paper — mountain fold down the center, valley fold at the crossbar. Softer than the solid blocks. "Folded from plan" — architectural origami.

Circuit

26
Idea: A drawn as PCB traces with 90° bends, solder pads at nodes, vias at transitions. Tech version of the blueprint idea. "Trace · solder · build."

Art Deco

27
Idea: Stepped Chrysler-building silhouette — layered triangles narrowing to a spire, with horizontal deco lines. 1920s optimism + vertical ambition.

Ribbon

28
Idea: A formed by a single continuous ribbon folding back on itself. V-notched ends, crease at apex, crossbar where ribbon crosses. "Woven together."

Typographic Mark

29
Idea: Custom serif A letterform as a pure typographic mark — strong axis, serif terminals, accent crossbar. Paired with a Didot-style wordmark. Elegant, editorial.

Letterpress

30
Idea: ARCHIFEX pressed into paper — emboss gradient for the impression, corner ornaments, classical divider. A nod to the physical craft of print before digital.
